We use AI more than ever — and lose more brilliant conversations than ever. Today we're opening up **KnowledgeMonkey**, the place we wished existed: an AI-native home for everything you learn, plan, and create with AI by your side.

## The problem we kept hitting

Every week, we'd have a chat with an LLM that solved a real problem — a clever prompt for a course outline, a deep technical answer, a perfectly-phrased argument. A week later? Gone. Buried in a thread, lost in a sidebar, never to return.

Linear chat is a brilliant interface for **exploring**. It is a terrible interface for **keeping**.

## What KnowledgeMonkey does

KnowledgeMonkey turns the artefacts of your AI life into a living second brain.

- **Capture** notes, voice memos, pasted answers and YouTube links — anything becomes a "knowledge chunk."
- **Organize** chunks into trees, mind maps or simple lists. Same data, three lenses.
- **Talk to it**, hands-free. Walking, driving, cooking — your brain comes with you.
- **Pick your LLM** per project: GPT, Claude, Perplexity, and a growing list.
- **Search semantically** — by meaning, not keywords. Scale to thousands of chunks without losing the thread.
- **Share** any project as read-only or open it up for contributors.
- **Export** everything, anytime, as Markdown, plain text or CSV. No lock-in. Ever.

## Built AI-native, not AI-bolted-on

A lot of apps are bolting "Ask AI" buttons onto a 2015 interface. We started from a blank page and asked: if an LLM had been in the loop from day one, what would the UI even look like?

The answer turned out to be: voice-first, chunk-shaped, multi-LLM, semantic-by-default, and yours to keep.

## Free forever core

The full core of KnowledgeMonkey is free, on every platform — web, iOS and Android. The only catch: on Free, you bring your own LLM API key. Pro removes that step with bundled cloud LLM credits, premium voices and advanced sharing.

## How to start

[Open the app](https://app.knowledgemonkey.ai) and tap "New project." Try one of the [flagship use cases](/#use-cases) — capture notes from your last AI chat, build a 10-lesson course on something you've always wanted to learn, or dictate a chapter on your next walk.

We'd love to see what you build with it.
